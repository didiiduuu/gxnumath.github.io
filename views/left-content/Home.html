the 4th National Conference on Time Series Analysis


Time series analysis, a key component of data science, plays an important role in fields such as signal processing, economics, climate change, environmental science and financial risk management. Its importance is even greater in the context of big data and machine learning. Recognising its pivotal role in China's development of science and technology, Professors Wang Shou-ren, An Hong-zhi and Chen Zhao-Guo, together with other distinguished statisticians of their generation, inaugurated the first National Conference on Time Series Analysis in Beijing in 1984, with Professor Howell Tong as overseas advisor. The conference was very successful, attracting nearly two hundred participants from all over China and several leading time series analysts, including Professors Emanual Parzen, George Tiao, Maurice Priestley, Ritei Shibata and Yosihiko Ogata.


Continuing this academic legacy, we are pleased to announce the 4th National Conference on Time Series Analysis to be held in Guilin from 1-7 July 2024.


This event will bring together distinguished experts to foster and accelerate the development of new analytical frameworks and methodologies, tailored to the complexity of big data in time series analysis. The conference aims to deepen our understanding of current challenges and stimulate innovative research. Last but not least, we would also like to take this opportunity to pay a special tribute to Professor Howell Tong (汤家豪), who will be speaking at the workshop, in recognition of his pioneering work in nonlinear time series analysis and his remarkable efforts and achievements in mentoring numerous world-class statisticians in China and around the world, some of whom will also be speaking at the conference.
